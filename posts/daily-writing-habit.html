Building a Habit of Daily Writing

June 15, 2026 · 6 min read

Every writing habit I've ever abandoned started the same way: with a system that was slightly too ambitious to survive a bad week. A thousand words a day. An hour before work. A streak counter I was terrified of breaking. All of them worked great for about three weeks, and then one missed day turned into a missed month.

The habit that actually lasted is embarrassingly small, and I think that's exactly why it lasted.

The rule: one paragraph, no exceptions

The only commitment is one paragraph a day, about anything. Not a thousand words. Not a finished thought. One paragraph, which on a good day turns into three pages and on a bad day is genuinely just one paragraph about how tired I am. Both count. Neither is a failure.

Making the minimum embarrassingly easy removes the only real threat to a daily habit, which isn't laziness — it's the all-or-nothing thinking that turns a busy Tuesday into a reason to quit entirely.

Why streaks made it worse, not better

I tried streak-tracking apps for a while. They're designed to make missing a day feel like losing something, and for me that backfired completely. The day I finally missed one, the streak resetting to zero felt like the habit itself had failed, so I stopped opening the app rather than face it.

A habit that punishes you for missing a day is a habit designed to be abandoned eventually.

Now I don't track anything. I just write the paragraph, wherever — a notes app, the back of a receipt, this site. The habit isn't a number going up. It's just something I do, the way you'd brush your teeth without counting consecutive days of dental hygiene.

What it's actually built

Most single paragraphs go nowhere and that's fine. But a few times a month, one of them turns out to be the seed of something — an argument I didn't know I had an opinion on, an image that's been sitting in the back of my head for a year. Those are the ones that eventually get pasted up here, cleaned up just enough to be readable.

The posts you see on this site are the rare paragraphs that turned into something. Underneath them is a much larger, much less impressive pile of one-paragraph days that never went anywhere — and that pile is the actual habit. The posts are just what's visible from the outside.
